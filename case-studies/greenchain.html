GreenChain Financed Emissions Tracking Tool

Co-founded a fintech startup focused on tracking financed emissions, raised USD 75k, and built a product supported by Google, Create-X & StartupBootCamp.

Organization GreenChain (Startup)
Role Co-Founder & Product Manager
Key Achievement USD 75k Raised
Supporters Google, Create-X, StartupBootCamp

Overview

GreenChain is a fintech platform that helps financial institutions track and manage their financed emissions—the carbon footprint associated with their investment and lending portfolios. As co-founder and Product Manager, I led product development from concept to MVP, successfully raised funding, and built a product that addresses a critical need in sustainable finance.

This project demonstrated my ability to build products from scratch, navigate the startup ecosystem, raise funding, and work with prestigious accelerators. It also showcased my passion for sustainability tech and my ability to translate complex financial and environmental concepts into user-friendly products.

Challenge

Building GreenChain presented several unique challenges:

  • Complex Domain: Financed emissions tracking requires deep understanding of both finance and environmental science
  • Regulatory Landscape: The field of sustainable finance is rapidly evolving with new regulations and standards
  • Data Challenges: Tracking emissions across diverse investment portfolios requires aggregating data from multiple sources
  • Market Education: Many potential customers were not yet aware of the need for financed emissions tracking
  • Resource Constraints: As a startup, we had limited resources and needed to be strategic about what to build
  • Funding: Needed to raise capital to build and scale the product

Approach

1. Market Research & Validation

I conducted extensive market research to validate the opportunity:

  • Customer Interviews: Spoke with 50+ financial institutions to understand their needs and challenges around emissions tracking
  • Competitive Analysis: Analyzed existing solutions to identify gaps and opportunities
  • Regulatory Research: Studied emerging regulations (TCFD, SFDR, etc.) to understand compliance requirements
  • Market Sizing: Estimated market size and growth potential

This research validated that there was a significant, underserved market for financed emissions tracking tools.

2. Product Strategy & MVP Definition

I defined the product strategy and MVP scope:

  • Value Proposition: Help financial institutions track, report, and reduce their financed emissions
  • Core Features: Portfolio emissions calculation, reporting dashboards, compliance tracking, reduction recommendations
  • MVP Scope: Focused on core emissions tracking and reporting to validate product-market fit
  • Technical Architecture: Designed scalable architecture to handle diverse portfolio data

3. Fundraising & Accelerator Programs

I led fundraising efforts and participated in prestigious accelerator programs:

  • Pitch Development: Created compelling pitch decks and investor materials
  • Investor Meetings: Conducted 30+ investor meetings to raise capital
  • Successfully Raised USD 75k: Secured funding from angel investors and early-stage funds
  • Google Support: Received support and mentorship from Google
  • Create-X Program: Participated in Georgia Tech's Create-X accelerator, receiving mentorship and resources
  • StartupBootCamp: Accepted into StartupBootCamp's fintech program, gaining access to mentors, investors, and partners

These programs provided not just funding, but also valuable mentorship, network access, and credibility in the startup ecosystem.

4. Product Development

I led product development from concept to MVP:

  • Requirements Definition: Created detailed PRDs for all MVP features
  • Data Integration: Worked with engineering team to integrate data from multiple sources (financial data providers, emissions databases)
  • Dashboard Design: Designed intuitive dashboards for emissions tracking and reporting
  • Compliance Features: Built features to help users comply with emerging regulations
  • User Testing: Conducted user testing with early customers to gather feedback and iterate

5. Go-to-Market Strategy

I developed and executed go-to-market strategy:

  • Customer Acquisition: Identified and reached out to target customers
  • Partnership Development: Built partnerships with data providers and consulting firms
  • Content Marketing: Created content to educate market about financed emissions
  • Industry Events: Participated in fintech and sustainability conferences to build brand awareness

Key Learnings

This startup experience taught me invaluable lessons:

  • Startup Experience is Valuable: Building a product from scratch teaches skills that are valuable in any product role—resourcefulness, prioritization, and customer focus
  • Fundraising is a Product: Raising capital requires the same skills as building products—understanding customer (investor) needs, clear communication, and demonstrating value
  • Accelerators Provide More Than Money: The mentorship, network, and resources from accelerators are often more valuable than the funding
  • Domain Expertise Matters: Deep understanding of the problem domain (sustainable finance) was essential for building a credible product
  • Customer Development is Continuous: In a new market, continuous customer development is essential to understand evolving needs
  • Passion Drives Persistence: Building a startup is hard—passion for the problem and mission is essential for persistence
