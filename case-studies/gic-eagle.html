EAGLE Performance Reporting Platform

Leading Agile delivery for GIC's enterprise performance management & reporting platform, improving monitoring, data integrity, and workflow automation.

Organization GIC (via Zenika)
Role Product Manager
Duration Ongoing
Key Tools Jira, Datadog, Control-M, Tableau

Overview

EAGLE is GIC's enterprise performance management and reporting platform, critical for monitoring investment performance, ensuring data integrity, and automating reporting workflows. As Product Manager, I led Agile delivery efforts to improve platform observability, optimize data pipelines, and streamline operations through automation.

This project required deep collaboration with data engineers, compliance teams, and business stakeholders to deliver improvements that enhanced both technical reliability and business value.

Challenge

The EAGLE platform faced several critical challenges:

  • Limited Observability: Control-M job failures and data pipeline issues were difficult to track and diagnose in real-time
  • Data Integrity Concerns: Manual processes and lack of automated validation led to potential data quality issues
  • Inefficient Workflows: Repetitive manual tasks in Jira and reporting processes consumed significant team time
  • Stakeholder Visibility: Business users needed better dashboards to understand system health and performance metrics

Approach

1. Enhanced Observability with Datadog

I designed and implemented comprehensive Datadog dashboards to provide real-time visibility into:

  • Control-M job execution status and failure rates
  • Data pipeline performance metrics
  • System resource utilization
  • Error rates and alerting thresholds

This enabled the team to proactively identify and resolve issues before they impacted business operations, reducing mean time to resolution (MTTR) by approximately 40%.

2. Data Integrity Improvements

I worked with data engineers to implement automated data validation checks and integrity monitoring:

  • Built SQL-based validation queries to check data completeness and accuracy
  • Created automated alerts for data quality anomalies
  • Established data lineage tracking to improve traceability
  • Reduced data integrity issues by 60% through proactive monitoring

3. Jira Automation Workflows

To streamline team workflows, I designed and implemented Jira automation rules that:

  • Automatically created tickets for Control-M job failures
  • Assigned tickets to appropriate team members based on job type
  • Updated ticket status based on external system events
  • Generated weekly status reports for stakeholders

These automations saved the team approximately 10 hours per week on manual ticket management.

4. Agile Delivery & Stakeholder Management

I facilitated sprint planning, daily standups, and retrospectives to ensure smooth delivery:

  • Managed product backlog with clear prioritization based on business impact
  • Conducted regular stakeholder demos to gather feedback
  • Coordinated with multiple engineering teams to ensure alignment
  • Maintained clear documentation in Confluence for knowledge sharing

Beyond the metrics, the improvements I delivered resulted in:

  • Improved Developer Experience: Engineers could quickly identify and resolve issues, leading to higher team satisfaction
  • Better Business Visibility: Stakeholders gained real-time insights into platform health through intuitive dashboards
  • Reduced Operational Risk: Proactive monitoring and automation reduced the likelihood of critical failures
  • Scalable Processes: Automation and observability improvements set the foundation for future platform growth

Key Learnings

This project reinforced several important lessons about product management in enterprise environments:

  • Observability is a Product Feature: Investing in monitoring and dashboards pays dividends in reduced downtime and faster issue resolution
  • Automation Creates Capacity: By automating repetitive tasks, we freed up team time for higher-value work
  • Data Quality is Non-Negotiable: In financial services, data integrity issues can have serious consequences—automated validation is essential
  • Stakeholder Communication Matters: Regular demos and clear documentation ensured alignment and built trust with business stakeholders

Technical Details

Technologies Used: Datadog (monitoring & dashboards), Jira (project management & automation), Control-M (job scheduling), SQL (data validation), Tableau (business dashboards), Confluence (documentation)

Methodology: Agile/Scrum with 2-week sprints, daily standups, sprint planning, and retrospectives

Team Structure: Cross-functional team including data engineers, DevOps engineers, QA engineers, and business analysts
